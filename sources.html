Provenance

Where the data comes from

Every node and every connection in this graph is grounded in NSF’s own public materials — not opinion or inference alone. Below is exactly what was used, and how each kind of relationship is sourced.

One rule: no connection is a hunch. Each relationship carries a specific, checkable source — a solicitation, an announcement quote, or an award record — and you can click any edge in the interactive graph to read it.
Programs & structure

NSF program solicitations & program pages

The 58 programs, their lifecycle status, eligibility, required partners, tracks/phases, and target audiences come from the full official solicitation pages and NSF program/initiative pages.

Directed & reference links

Official announcements & Dear Colleague Letters

Directed pathways and solicitation references are drawn from explicit language in NSF announcements (e.g. “open to current and former SBIR/STTR awardees”) — each carries a verbatim quote.

Shared researchers

NSF award records

Award co-occurrence — when two programs funded the same people or institutions — is computed from NSF’s public award data, then size-normalized so high-volume programs don’t dominate.

Technology areas

Statutory Key Technology Areas

The 10 Key Technology Areas come from the CHIPS and Science Act, mapped to programs using NSF’s own technology tagging where available and program descriptions otherwise.

How relationships are graded

Each connection records how it was established, so you always know whether a human checked it:

  • Curated — directed pathways, each accepted in review against a quoted source. No quote, no arrow.
  • Validated — solicitation references, verified against the official announcement text.
  • Auto-extracted — operational, award-co-occurrence, text-similarity, and shared-domain links, generated from structured fields and shown with their evidence and confidence.
A note on independence. This is an independent research visualization built from publicly available information. It is not an official NSF product and is not endorsed by NSF. For authoritative program information, always refer to nsf.gov.
